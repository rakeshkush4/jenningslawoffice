## The Threshold for Medical Malpractice in South Carolina

Medical malpractice is a complex legal matter. To begin, the courts recognize that people make mistakes, and this includes those in the medical profession. For a doctor’s actions to qualify as medical malpractice, they must have gone beyond the boundaries that other medical professionals would have under similar circumstances. This can mean failing to provide the level of care that other reasonably prudent medical professionals would have or engaging in a practice that other reasonably prudent medical professionals wouldn’t have.

Proving that a medical professional deviated from professional standards to this degree is a serious undertaking. Doing so generally requires having the testimony of expert witnesses who can speak knowledgeably regarding the medical professional’s actions or inactions in relation to the relevant circumstances.

## The Kinds of Incidents that Often Support Medical Malpractice Claims

Your medical malpractice claim will be unique to you and your situation, but most medical malpractice cases fall into categories like the following:


- Failing to diagnose or failing to treat, including failing to adequately treat infections
- Misdiagnosis
- Errors related to surgery, including anesthesia errors
- Birth injuries – to either the newborn child or to the mother
- Prescription errors

Beyond these primary classifications, there are also what are termed [never events](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK499956/){:target="_blank" rel="noopener"}, and they refer to errors that should never happen but sometimes do, including:


- Performing surgery on the wrong patient
- Performing the wrong surgery
- Performing the right surgery but on the wrong body part, such as the left knee instead of the right
- Leaving a medical tool or implement inside the patient

## Your Full Range of Legal Damages

If you’ve been harmed by medical malpractice, you’re entitled to just [compensation](/verdicts-and-settlements/) in each of the following areas:


- The medical expenses you incur, which include those related to the health complications you experience.
- Your lost wages, which may need to address a long absence from work and serious losses in relation to earning potential.
- The physical and emotional pain and suffering you endure as a result of the medical professional’s negligence, which South Carolina caps.

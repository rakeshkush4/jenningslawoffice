## The Lyft Driver’s Negligence

Lyft drivers are just as likely to engage in driver negligence as anyone else on the road is, and they may be more so. The fact is that Lyft drivers earn money behind the wheel, and as a result, the pressure to perform can be considerable. The kinds of driver negligence that are most closely associated with Lyft accidents include all the following:


- Speeding, which makes accidents more likely and makes them more likely to be deadly.
- Distracted driving, which translates to paying inadequate attention to the road ahead and can lead to very dangerous accidents.
- Impairment, which is very closely associated with deadly traffic accidents.
- Exhaustion, which comes with impairments of its own and, is considered a primary factor in many car accidents.

## How You’ll File Your Lyft Accident Claim

If a Lyft driver leaves you injured in an accident, how you file your claim will be determined by the step in the Lyft process that they were in at the time. Consider the following:


- If you were a passenger in the Lyft vehicle, if the Lyft driver had another passenger, or if the Lyft driver was on their way to pick up a passenger at the time, your claim will go through [Lyft’s policy](https://www.lyft.com/driver/insurance){:target="_blank" rel="noopener"}.
- If the Lyft driver was waiting to be assigned a passenger, you’ll file your claim with their personal policy, which Lyft requires them to carry, but any excess amount – that exceeds the driver’s coverage limits – will go through Lyft.
- If the Lyft driver was toggled off the app at the time of the accident, your claim will go through the driver’s policy – just like it would after any other car accident.

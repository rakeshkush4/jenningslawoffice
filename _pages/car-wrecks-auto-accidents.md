## Driver Negligence

Most car accident claims are caused by negligence on the part of an involved driver, and this negligence can take all the following very dangerous forms:

- Impairment
- Distraction
- Exhaustion
- Failure to follow the safety rules of the road, such as failing to yield the right-of-way as required or failure to obey traffic lights, signs, or signals
- Speeding, which includes driving too fast in response to the road’s condition or the dangerous effects of bad weather
- Aggression

## Proving the Other Driver’s Negligence

Proving that the other driver caused the accident that left you injured will come down to evidence like the following, which speaks to their responsibility:

- Eyewitness testimony
- Your own account of how the accident happened
- Photos and footage captured by people at the scene or by dashcams, security cameras, or traffic cameras in the vicinity
- Physical evidence, such as the positions in which the vehicles landed, skid marks on the road, paint transfer from vehicle to vehicle, and beyond
- Professional accident reconstruction models that depict the mechanics of the accident
- The testimony of professionals with expertise in accident-related fields
- The police report, including any tickets that were issued, such as for drunk driving

You can trust your [focused](/firm-overview/) car accident lawyer to take the steps necessary to clearly demonstrate the other driver’s negligence in your claim.

## Your Physical, Financial, and Emotional Losses

In your car accident claim, you will seek fair compensation for your losses in each of the following important categories:

- Your medical costs, which can be extensive and may show no signs of subsiding any time soon
- Your lost income, which may include losses that relate to your earning power
- Your physical and emotional pain and suffering, which can outpace every other form of loss you experience combined

## When You Are on Your Bike

When you take to the streets on your bike, you make a healthy choice that is also good for the environment, but in the process, you are exceptionally vulnerable to the negligence of drivers with whom you share the road. You do not have the protective shell of a car’s exterior to shield you from the immense impact of an accident, and the fact that you take up very little space on the road makes it easier for distracted drivers to fail to see you all together. In other words, you are at greater risk when you are on your bike, and bike accidents are often very serious.

## Bike Laws In South Carolina

In South Carolina, bicyclists are held to the same rules and regulations that motorists are, which means following all the safety rules of the road and riding in the same direction as traffic. Additional rules that apply include all the following:


- You should ride to the rightmost part of the road as it is practical.
- When there is a bike lane, you should use it – except when you need to make a left turn, you need to pass another bicyclist, or there is an obstruction that prevents you from using it.
- Never ride more than two abreast when riding in a group.
- If you ride after dark, make sure your bike is equipped with the lights and reflectors you need to be seen.

These [bike safety rules](https://scdps.sc.gov/bicyclesafety){:target="_blank" rel="noopener"} are shared by the South Carolina Department of Public Safety.

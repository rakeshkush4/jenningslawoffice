## Common Personal Injury Claims 

Personal injury claims come in many different forms, but most fall into primary categories like the following:


- Car, truck, bike, motorcycle, pedestrian, and rideshare accidents, including accidents caused by impaired or distracted drivers
- Premises liability claims, including slip and fall accidents as well as dog bites
- Product liability claims
- Accidents leading to catastrophic injuries, including traumatic brain injuries (TBIs)

Wrongful death claims and medical malpractice cases are guided by laws that are very similar to personal injury laws, and they are often grouped together.

## The Elements of Your Claim

To recover compensation for your losses in a personal injury claim of any kind, each of the following elements must be accounted for:

- You were owed a duty of care, which means that the other person or party had a responsibility for your safety. A prime example of this is the responsibility every driver accepts when they get behind the wheel.
- The other party breached the duty of care they owed you, which means they engaged in some form of negligence. When it comes to traffic accidents, impairment and [distraction](https://www.nhtsa.gov/risky-driving/distracted-driving){:target="_blank" rel="noopener"} are common examples. 
- Their negligence was the direct cause of the accident in question.
- You suffered legal damages – or losses that personal injury law addresses – as a result.

Your dedicated personal injury lawyer has the legal insight and drive to ensure that each of these elements is well established.

## Recovering on Your Damages

In your personal injury claim, you will pursue compensation for the damages you suffer as a result of the other party’s negligence in each of the following categories that apply:

- Property damage, such as to your vehicle in a traffic accident.
- Medical expenses, which – if you were seriously injured – may be ongoing, may need to address future healthcare needs, and may involve health complications or secondary concerns.
- Lost earnings, which could include lost earning potential and an altered career trajectory.
- Physical and emotional pain and suffering, which can amplify your other losses and overshadow them in terms of overall impact.

A critical component of your personal injury claim is proving the full range of losses you’ve suffered as a result of the other party’s negligence, and your [practiced](/firm-overview/)  personal injury lawyer will leave no stone unturned toward this end.

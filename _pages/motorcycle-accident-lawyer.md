## Risk Factors to Keep in Mind

When you’re driving a motorcycle, you have all the power of the other vehicles on the road, but you’re far more difficult to see, which translates to increased risk. Consider all the following prime examples of risk factors you face:


- Drivers can have a more difficult time seeing you and gauging your speed, which can leave you especially vulnerable in intersections.
- You’re much more likely to get lost in other drivers’ blind spots than most of the other motorists on the road are, and when a motorist fails to take the necessary precautions before changing lanes, you can pay the ultimate price. 
- You require much less stopping distance than other drivers do, and as a result, you’re more likely to be rear-ended.
- You're more susceptible to the dangers associated with poorly maintained roads and bad weather.

When you’re on your bike, safety should always be your focus.

## The Negligence of Other Drivers

Every form of driver negligence out there – and there are many – puts motorcyclists like you at increased risk of being injured. Some of the most common and the most dangerous include all the following:


- Distracted driving
- Drowsy driving
- Failing to follow all the rules of the road
- Driving too fast in relation to the posted speed limit or in relation to the road’s condition
- Aggressive driving
- [Drunk driving](https://www.nhtsa.gov/risky-driving/drunk-driving){:target="_blank" rel="noopener"}

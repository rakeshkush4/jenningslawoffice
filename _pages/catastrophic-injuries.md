## Is It a Catastrophic Injury?

To be classified as catastrophic, your injury must lead to a long-term or permanent disability or disfigurement or must interfere with your quality of life to a significant degree. Catastrophic injuries tend to require extensive medical care that is likely to include rehabilitation, therapeutic care, and ongoing healthcare needs – and may require assistance with the activities of daily living. In other words, a catastrophic injury can alter the course of your life in mere moments, and obtaining the compensation to which you’re entitled will play a critical role in your recovery.

## Injuries that Are Frequently Classified as Catastrophic

If the injury you suffer is serious enough to cause a lasting impairment, it can be classified as catastrophic. There are certain kinds of injuries, however, that frequently reach this threshold.

**Spinal Cord Injuries**


Serious spinal cord injuries can permanently affect your range of motion, and they are closely associated with debilitating chronic pain. If your spinal cord is severed or crushed, you’ll face permanent paralysis, which translates to a lifetime of loss.

**Traumatic Brain Injuries**


[Traumatic brain injuries](https://www.cdc.gov/traumatic-brain-injury/index.html){:target="_blank" rel="noopener"} can lead to life-altering health and emotional consequences that derail the course of your future. Many victims of these very challenging injuries experience a shift in their sense of self that is especially difficult to handle.

**Limb Loss and Injuries that Lead to Amputation**


Losing a limb or having a limb amputated as a result of someone else’s negligence is a terrible blow that is associated with serious losses in relation to mobility and immense physical and emotional pain and suffering.

**Serious Burns and Other Forms of Disfigurement**


Severe burns are among the most painful injuries you can endure, and they often lead to permanent disfigurement. Burn victims require specialized care that tends to be ongoing, and serious complications are common.

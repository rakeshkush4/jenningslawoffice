Construction sites are inherently unsafe, because they change every day. Though workers and visitors are entitled to a reasonably safe environment, accidents continue to happen. Some of them are very serious and include such catastrophic injuries as:

- Paralysis and other spinal injuries
- Amputations
- Crushing by vehicles and machines
- Asphyxiation
- Lacerations
- Explosions
- Severe burns
- Chemical toxifications

## **Experienced Premises Liability Lawyers**

Some construction site accidents and other premises liability accidents are due to objects falling, or people falling. Some are classic slip-and-falls and trip-and-falls. Some involve work infrastructure such as scaffolds and cranes. Many occur because of poor signage, defective products and workers insufficiently trained in safety procedures.

It is not unusual for construction workers to work in precarious locations without the proper safety equipment. Our firm has experience in obtaining compensation for clients who have been injured at construction sites or on others’ premises due to a variety of defects, including a lack of safety equipment, unstable scaffolds and defective machinery.

Uber has opened up a world of opportunity when it comes to getting around, but if you’re injured in an Uber accident, it amplifies the complications you face in relation to your traffic accident claim. Uber drivers earn a living behind the wheel, but they aren’t professional drivers in any other sense, and the stress and distractions of the work can increase the risk that accidents will happen. If you or someone you care about has been injured in an Uber accident, discuss the matter with an experienced Bennettsville [Uber accident attorney](/) as soon as you’re able to do so.

## Uber Drivers Face Additional Challenges

Uber drivers get paid by the number of trips they complete, which means there is pressure to perform. In the process, they experience a wide range of distractions, including all the following:


- They have to interact with the app as they drive.
- They often have to navigate areas that they may not be familiar with.
- They must be on the lookout for passengers whom they’ve never seen before but are waiting to be picked up.
- They need to locate spots that are safe for dropping off and picking up passengers.
- They’re required to enter and exit traffic often and to make frequent stops and starts, which increases the risk that accidents will occur.

## Your Uber Accident Claim

If you’re injured by an Uber driver’s negligence, the matter of how you’ll file your claim depends on where the driver was in the Uber process at the time, which will play out in one of the following ways:


- If they had a passenger or were on their way to pick one up, you’ll file your claim with Uber’s provider, and you can expect [comprehensive coverage](https://www.uber.com/us/en/drive/insurance/){:target="_blank" rel="noopener"}.
- If they were on the Uber app but were waiting for a fare to be assigned, you’ll file your claim with the policy that Uber requires them to carry, and if that isn’t sufficient to cover your complete losses, you’ll also file a claim with Uber’s policy.
- If they were no longer on the app – even if they just hopped off – your claim will proceed just like it would after any other car accident. This means filing your claim with the driver’s personal provider.

The most important point to make here is that, regardless of the insurance company – or companies – involved, you can expect them to do everything in their power to keep your settlement as low as possible. This makes working closely with a resourceful Uber accident lawyer the best course of action.

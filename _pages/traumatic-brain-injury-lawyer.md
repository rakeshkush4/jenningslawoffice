## The Emotional Consequences of a Traumatic Brain Injury

Traumatic brain injuries (TBIs) are exceptionally dangerous, and they also tend to pack a serious emotional punch. Many victims of TBIs suffer symptoms that are not dissimilar to PTSD, including all the following difficult consequences:


- Difficulty controlling one’s emotions
- Severe mood swings
- Sleep disturbances that can include night terrors
- Increased anxiety that can include panic attacks
- Terrifying accident flashbacks
- A tendency to push others away, including those attempting to offer their support
- Difficulty concentrating

The impact of a TBI can be all-encompassing and very difficult to move past.

## Health Outcomes

TBIs are very closely associated with traffic crashes and falls – especially from heights – and the health outcomes for those who experience moderate to severe TBIs are quite grim. Consider the following statistics for those who are at the five-year mark post-accident:


- Only about 26 percent show any improvement.
- About 30 percent are worse off.
- Approximately 22 percent have [died](/personal-injury-overview/wrongful-death/).
- Around 22 percent remain the same.

These numbers are shared by the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC), and they emphasize the overall losses that affect not only victims of traumatic brain injuries but also their loved ones and society at large.

## Your Physical, Financial, and Emotional Losses

Many people who suffer TBIs require ongoing care to help them reach their most complete recoveries possible – given their unique circumstances. The categories of loss you can seek just compensation for in a traumatic brain injury claim include the following:


- Medical expenses, which will likely need to cover ongoing care, such as various therapies and rehabilitation, and may include the need for assistance with the activities of daily living
- Lost income, which can be immense and may reflect an inability to work at all
- Physical and emotional pain and suffering, which can be difficult to overstate in the face of an injury this serious

Your focused traumatic brain injury lawyer will put the time and effort necessary into proving the full scope of your complete losses.
